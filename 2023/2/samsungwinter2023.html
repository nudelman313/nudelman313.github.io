Samsung Galaxy Unpacked to Reveal Galaxy S23 Phones Today

by Matthew Nudelman
Month DD, YYYY

Today, Samsung is set to hold its annual Samsung Unpacked event to present their newest Galaxy S phones, the Samsung Galaxy S23 series. A little bit of information on the phones has already come out, though some of it has yet to be officially confirmed. This includes information about the three different models that will be featured, following Samsung's trend of having three models: a normal phone, a “plus” phone, and their flagship “ultra” phone. Similar to Google, Samsung is not expected to drastically change the design of their phones this year, as they seem to have found their current identity in the look and feel of the Galaxy S22 phones just as Google did with the Pixel 6 phones.
The event will begin at 1:00 pm EST today, February 1, and is expected to showcase a couple of different things, specifically the Galaxy S23 phones and a new Galaxy Book.
The Samsung Galaxy S23 is expected to have a near-identical appearance to the S22, though many rumors strongly suggest that the rear camera corner will no longer have a raised edge as the S21 and S22 did, following in the footsteps of the Galaxy S22 Ultra and unifying the looks of the phones across the board. The cameras themselves are said to be the same, a 50MP primary sensor with a 12MP ultrawide and 10MP telephoto. The display will also be the same, a 6.1-inch FHD+ screen, which is definitely no disappointment as Samsung is a prolific provider of high-quality display panels even to their competitors (including Apple, whose iPhones use Samsung displays). The cameras in the S23+ will be the same as the S23, so they are not going to be different either. The main upgrade the phones are expected to have is in the processor, as it is believed that both the S23 and S23+ will be using the new Snapdragon 8 Gen 2, not just in certain regions, but everywhere in the world. This would be unexpected as Samsung typically uses their own Exynos chips for certain regions, usually leading to backlash as the Exynos phones tend to be significantly underpowered despite the pricing being relatively the same as the Snapdragon phones. This upgrade to the Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 would be very significant, especially in regions that normally get stuck with Exynos phones. However, the overall price of the S23 series is expected to increase, though a similar rumor surrounding the price of the iPhone 14 was eventually proven to be untrue at the September 2022 Apple Event. As expected, the Galaxy S23 Ultra is the biggest point of interest, with its expected 200MP main sensor to replace the 108MP sensor of the camera system of the highly-praised S22 Ultra. The S-Pen is also expected to stay as the S23 Ultra is said to be moving forward with the Ultra redesign of 2022. The device will potentially have slightly less rounded edges to make holding the phone and using the S-Pen more comfortable.
The Samsung Unpacked event will go live 1pm EST, 12pm CST, and 10am PST today, Feb 1, 2023, and it will definitely be interesting to see what sorts of changes may be made to Samsung's main series of smartphones.
